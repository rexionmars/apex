\chapter{Dashboard and export}

\eyebrow{track the sample, ship the dataset}

\vspace{4pt}
The \textbf{Project dashboard} (Figure~\ref{fig:dashboard}) is the overview of the
whole effort. It shows how far the sample has progressed, how well the raters
agree, and whether the pairing is intact. It also holds the button that produces
the final dataset.

\shot{dashboard.jpeg}{The Project dashboard. Summary cards (carcasses, images,
graded, pairing); the stratified-sample progress toward the target; inter-rater
agreement; and the export panel with the integrity report.}{dashboard}

\section{Sample progress}

Summary cards show the totals and the pairing status (\pillok{intact} when no
image is orphaned). The \textbf{Stratified sample (R3)} panel tracks progress
toward the target of 100--120 carcasses, broken down per batch and stratum, so
under-represented strata are visible.

\section{Inter-rater agreement}

The \textbf{Fleiss' $\kappa$} panel reports agreement per axis (conformation,
finishing), for all batches or a chosen one. Until at least two raters have scored
a common set of carcasses it reads \tele{$\kappa$ not computable yet}.

\section{Export dataset}

The \textbf{Export dataset} button produces two files in a chosen folder:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabularx}{\linewidth}{@{}l X@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{File} & \textbf{Contents} \\
\midrule
\tele{manifest.csv} & One row per image, with the full pairing: physical tag,
      animal, treatment, stratum, consensus grades, agreement, fat percentage,
      conformation indices and the physical reference. \\
\tele{integrity\_report.txt} & A human-readable attestation: totals per stratum,
      any orphan images excluded, inter-rater agreement, and file hashes. \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabularx}
\end{center}

Only carcasses with verified pairing are included; optionally, only those with a
valid consensus grade. The integrity report is the artefact that attests the
2022 failure did not recur.
