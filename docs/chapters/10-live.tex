\chapter{Live monitor}

\eyebrow{real-time fat overlay for framing}

\vspace{4pt}
The \textbf{Live monitor} shows a fat overlay in real time, from a camera or a
video file, to help you frame the carcass while capturing. It is a
\emph{framing guide}, not the official measurement.

\section{Using it}

\shot{live-monitor.jpeg}{The Live monitor, stopped. A source switch (Live camera /
Video file) chooses the frame source; \tele{Start} begins the overlay and
\tele{Capture background} records the empty scene for subtraction. The engine
state and device are shown at the top.}{live-monitor}

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.4em,itemsep=3pt]
  \item Choose the source: \textbf{Live camera} or \textbf{Video file}.
  \item Press \tele{Start}. The video plays and the fat overlay (cyan) tracks it,
        with a live FPS and fat-percentage readout in the corner.
  \item For a fixed camera, press \tele{Capture background} with the scene empty;
        the app subtracts it from each frame so the overlay is restricted to the
        carcass. Then position the carcass.
\end{enumerate}

\section{How it works, and its limits}

The monitor uses classical background subtraction (fast, $\sim$10\,ms) plus the
fat model at reduced resolution, reaching roughly real-time rates on the tested
hardware. It deliberately does \emph{not} use the precise (but slow) background
removal of the Analysis pipeline.

\begin{caution}[title={Framing guide, not the validated number}]
The live overlay is approximate, a visual aid to get the framing right. The
validated fat number comes from the Analysis tab, run on a captured image with
precise background removal.
\end{caution}
